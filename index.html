What are dental veneers, and what do they do?


Dental veneers are thin porcelain shells bonded to the front of your teeth. They are used to improve the appearance of your smile by changing the color, shape, and size of your teeth. Veneers can also be used to fix chips or cracks in your teeth and to close gaps between your teeth.

If you are considering getting dental veneers, you must consult a dentist to see if they are the right option for you. The following are some things to keep in mind when considering dental veneers:

The color of your veneers can match the color of your natural teeth. Veneers are not reversible, and they will require some preparation of your natural teeth.

You will need to take care of your veneers just like your natural teeth, including brushing and flossing regularly.

How are dental veneers placed on teeth, and what is the process like?


Dental veneers are usually placed on the teeth in two visits. During the first visit, the dentist will take an impression of your teeth and send it to a dental laboratory. The dental laboratory will then create custom-made veneers specific to your teeth. Once the veneers are ready, you will need to come back for a second visit. During this visit, the dentist will place the veneers on your teeth and make any necessary adjustments. The entire process usually takes about two weeks.

What are the risks associated with getting dental veneers placed on your teeth?

There are very few risks associated with getting dental veneers. Most people who get them placed on their teeth experience no problems whatsoever. However, as with any type of medical procedure, a small number of people may experience some complications. The most common complication is that the veneer may not bond properly to the tooth. This can cause the veneer to come loose and eventually fall off. In sporadic cases, the veneer may damage the tooth, but this is usually only seen in cases where the veneer was not placed correctly in the first place. Overall, dental veneers are an extremely safe and effective way to improve the appearance of your teeth.
